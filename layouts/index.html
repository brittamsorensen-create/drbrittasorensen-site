The Research Focus

The questions that matter — and are often overlooked

How do female athletes experience pain differently?

Pain perception and tolerance in ultra-endurance sport is not gender-neutral. My PhD research explored biopsychosocial pain management strategies specific to female athletes — filling a critical gap in the literature.

What mental strategies actually work at the sharp end?

When the body is screaming and the finish line is still 100km away, which psychological tools make the difference? Evidence-based answers, not generic advice.

Why is this community still underserved by research?

Female ultra-endurance athletes are a distinct population with unique physiology, psychology, and lived experience. I am committed to changing the research landscape — and making findings accessible.

About Britta

Researcher. Athlete. Advocate.

I am Dr Britta Sorensen — applied sport psychology researcher, ultra cyclist, triathlete, and sport massage therapist based in south-west France. My PhD examined how female ultra-endurance athletes manage pain and discomfort through a biopsychosocial lens: the findings challenge assumptions and open new possibilities for how we support this remarkable group of athletes.

What drives me is the gap between what research tells us and what athletes actually experience. I have lived both sides — completing ultra-endurance events myself while studying the psychology behind why we push through, and how we can do so more effectively.

This site is where I bring those worlds together: rigorous research made accessible, practical resources for athletes and practitioners, and a growing community of people committed to advancing the science and support of female ultra-endurance sport.
